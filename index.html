Tom Jobim

An enlightened man

A brief story of his life...

  • Antônio Carlos Brasileiro de Almeida Jobim was born on January 27, 1927 in the Tijuca neighborhood of Rio de Janeiro.
  • 1935 - With the death of his father, his mother Nilza marries Celso da Frota Pessoa who encourages him in his musical career.
  • 1941 - He began studying music with the German Hans-Joachim Koellreutter.
  • Mid-40s - Starts playing piano in bars and nightclubs in Rio de Janeiro.
  • Early 50s - Works as an arranger at Estúdio Continental.
  • 1953 - Records his first song, "Incerteza", with lyrics by Newton Mendonça.
  • 1956 - Composes the music for the play "Orfeu da Conceição". The song that had the most prominence was "Se todos fossem iguais a você" with lyrics by Vinicius de Moraes.
  • 1958 - João Gilberto releases his first album with some songs by Tom Jobim and the term Bossa Nova appears.
  • 1962 - The famous performance takes place at Carnegie Hall that begins the launch of Bossa Nova throughout the world.
  • 1964 - Releases the LP "The Composer of Desafinado, Plays".
  • 1964 - Releases the LP "Antônio Carlos Jobim".
  • 1965 - Releases the LP "A certain Mr. Jobim".
  • 1965 - The song "The girl from Ipanema" wins the award for best recording of the year.
  • 1967 - Releases the LP "Wave".
  • 1970 - Releases the LP "Stone Flower".
  • 1970 - Releases the LP "Tide".
  • 1973 - Releases the LP "Matita Perê".
  • 1976 - Releases the LP "Urubu".
  • 1980 - Releases the LP "Terra Brasilis".
  • 1987 - Releases the LP "Passarim".
  • 1994 - He died on December 8.
  • 1994 - Three days after his death, the LP "Antonio Brasileiro" was released.
